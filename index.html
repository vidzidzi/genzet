What is the Gmail Dot Trick?

The Gmail Dot Trick is a clever technique that leverages a specific feature of Google's email service. Gmail's servers completely ignore any periods (dots) placed in the username part of an email address.

For example, if your primary address is yourname@gmail.com, emails sent to your.name@gmail.com, y.o.u.r.n.a.m.e@gmail.com will all arrive in your main inbox.

Common Uses

  • Filtering: Create powerful filters to organize emails.
  • Spam Identification: Discover which service sold your email.
  • Multiple Accounts: Register multiple accounts with one inbox.
